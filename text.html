The Nile-Delta

The Nile-Delta is something truly remarkable. Spanning across approximately 24.000 km², it is the most fertile soil in Northern-Africa. The juicy green land, in the characteristic triangle shape, can be seen from way above in the orbit and really pops out in the sandy and droughty Sahara-Area. Before it arrives here the Nile passes over 5000 Kilometers from the Lake Victoria in Central Africa The Nile represents the lifeline of Egypt. The two biggest cities of the country Cairo and Alexandria are located at the Delta and overall 60 Million People live here.

This can be seen especially when swiping to the night layer. Almost all the Lights of Egypt are emitted exactly along the Route of the Nile. In the Delta itself the lights get very bright. At Times when the Roman Empire still existed there were more than seven arms of water building the Delta, today after the Assuan-Dam was built only two streams remain and new land for further settlement was created. For your next holiday it might be interesting as well that many antique historical sites are located within the Delta.

The Korean Border

After the Korean-War in 1953 when the peninsula became two separated countries the two parties agreed on a demilitarised zone dividing the peninsula in a Northern and Southern part. This Border is not just a single line on a map represented with a fence in the real world. This Border is an effective barrier almost 250 kilometres long and about 4 kilometers wide and seperates the two sides. Nobody is allowed to enter this zone and this is supervised by the Military of both countries but there exists a small joint Security Area called Panmunjeom, where the armed truce was signed. While no humans were there on this strip of land for several decades now, nature has done its thing and created a jungle there. It is reported that Tigers and other endangered species found refuge there.

Almost astonishing is the fact that also by Night one can clearly identify this Borderline. This is because on the South-Korean-Side of the Border the villages reach as far as the edge of this zone. The countryside just north of this jungle is not so much populated. Futhermore South-Korea is wealthier than North-Korea in aspects such as public street-lighting in villages and advanced lighting technologies.
Did you know that some Initiatives suggest that this demilitarized natural zone should become a UNESCO-World-Heritage in the future.

Australia's Outback

Australia, the biggest Island of the World and therefore also an own continent. With an area of 7.600.000 km² it is the sixth largest country in the world. Australia is not densely populated, only about 25 Million inhabitants liver there, which is far less than in the Nile-Delta for example. Interesting is the fact that Australia lies within different climatological zones, which results in the fact that in the center of this island there exists a huge desert, the Australian Outback. The red shining Ayers Rock, a landmark in Australia, is located there. In the Outback it can get very hot during daytime and freezing cold during nightnime, so not very liveable conditions.

Therefore, and this can be seen especially in the night layer, all big Australian cities are located on the coast. Sydney, Melbourne, Adelaide, Perth and Brisbane, so all relevant light emitting clusters, can be found next to the ocean. Only the capital Canberra is located upcountry. The Coast Line is more than 25.000 kilometres long and very popular by surfers.
Another fact you might did not know is that Australia is divided by three time-zones, so pack your watch and don’t forget your surfboard.

Metropolis Moscow

Moscow is the capital of Russia and more than 15 Million people call it their hometown. The City consists of 12 administrative districts. The Ostankino-Television-Tower in Moscow is the highest building in Europe with 540 meters. Eight main train stations, four international airports and three domestic ports and several large boulevards make the city the most important transportation-hub in the Country. Furthermore you can find more than 300 Churches in the City. The Population density is more than 4500 inhabitants per square kilometre.

When switching to the night layer, you can notice not only is this a huge city but also the largest agglomeration of Europe. In the form of a star and along important transport axis, the suburbs are gathered and arranged. Far off from the center is still light visible. Together with the famous ring-motorway around the city, it looks a little bit like a spiders-web when looking from the sky. Around the city there is also a lot of area covered by woods. Known is Moscow also for the cultural offers in the city, such as operas and ballet-Theatres. The centre of the city forms the red square where also the iconic Basilius-Cathedral is located. Seems like definitely worth a visit.

Try to find and identify other Big European Cities like London, Paris, Madrid or Berlin in the night layer.
